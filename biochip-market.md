<p><p>The biochip market encompasses various technologies, primarily microarrays and microfluidics. Microarrays are used for high-throughput analysis, allowing simultaneous examination of multiple genetic sequences, proteins, or other biomolecules. They enable applications like gene expression profiling and genotyping. In contrast, microfluidics involves manipulating small volumes of fluids in microscale channels, facilitating rapid and precise biochemical reactions. This technology is essential for applications such as diagnostic testing and drug development, offering increased efficiency and reduced costs in biomedical research.</p></p>
