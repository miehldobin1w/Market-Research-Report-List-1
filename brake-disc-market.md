<p><p>The brake disc market primarily includes two types: cast iron and carbon matrix composite (CMC). Cast iron discs are widely used due to their affordability and reliable performance, offering good heat dissipation and durability. In contrast, CMC brake discs are engineered for high-performance applications, providing superior heat resistance, lower weight, and enhanced braking efficiency. While cast iron remains the mainstream choice, the growing demand for lightweight and advanced materials in automotive and aerospace sectors is driving interest in CMC technology.</p></p>
