<p><p>The Inorganic Advanced Phase Change Materials (PCM) market is categorized into two primary types: metallic and non-metallic materials. Metallic PCMs, such as paraffin waxes, offer high thermal conductivity and enhanced structural stability, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. Non-metallic PCMs, including salts and salt hydrates, are known for their excellent thermal energy storage capabilities and cost-effectiveness. Both types are used in various sectors, including construction, electronics, and thermal management systems, to improve energy efficiency and thermal regulation.</p></p>
